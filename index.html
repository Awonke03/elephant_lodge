Our Story

Born from a Love
of the Wild

Elephant Lodge was founded in 2008 by conservationist Amara Dube, who dreamed of a space where guests could connect deeply with nature — without sacrificing comfort or conscience.

Situated on 12,000 acres of private reserve bordering the greater Limpopo ecosystem, we share land with over 400 elephants, the Big Five, and hundreds of bird species.

Every rand you spend here directly funds our wildlife anti-poaching and habitat restoration programmes.

Who We Are

About
Elephant Lodge

Where conservation meets luxury hospitality in the Limpopo wilderness.

Our Background

Elephant Lodge was established in 2008 on the banks of the Limpopo River, South Africa. What began as a small eco-camp has grown into an award-winning luxury safari destination, consistently ranked among Africa's Top 10 Safari Lodges by Safari Digest and Condé Nast Traveller.

Our 12,000-acre private reserve is home to free-roaming elephant herds, lion prides, leopard, rhino, buffalo, and over 350 bird species. Conservation is at the core of every decision we make.

Our Mission

To provide world-class, sustainable safari experiences that deepen guests' connection with Africa's wilderness — while actively contributing to the conservation and protection of endangered wildlife and their habitats.

We believe that responsible tourism is one of the most powerful forces for lasting wildlife conservation on the continent. Every booking funds our work in the field.

Our Vision

A future where wild elephants roam freely across expanded, fully protected corridors — and where every visitor to Elephant Lodge leaves as a passionate, lifelong ambassador for African wildlife conservation.

By 2030, we aim to expand our reserve by 5,000 acres and achieve fully carbon-neutral lodge operations.

Our Target Audience

We welcome nature-loving travellers who seek meaningful, immersive experiences over mass tourism. Our typical guest values authenticity, sustainability, and the privilege of encountering wildlife on its own terms.

We cater to couples, families, solo explorers, wildlife photographers, and small corporate retreats — all united by a deep curiosity about the natural world.

"Leave nothing but footprints. Take nothing but memories."

Elephant Lodge operates under a strict low-impact philosophy. Our tented suites are constructed from reclaimed timber and locally sourced materials. We run on 80% solar power, use greywater recycling systems, and source 90% of food from nearby farms and our own lodge garden.

Ten percent of all revenue is reinvested directly into our wildlife anti-poaching unit, supporting 18 full-time rangers who protect the reserve around the clock, every day of the year.

Safari Options

Choose Your Adventure

Expert-guided experiences tailored to your pace and passion for the wild.

Most Popular

Big Five Game Drive

3–4 hours · Morning & Afternoon departures

Track lion, leopard, elephant, rhino, and buffalo with expert rangers in an open-sided Land Cruiser. Includes a sundowner drinks stop at a scenic bush viewpoint.

  • FGASA-certified guide
  • Binoculars & field guides provided
  • Morning coffee or afternoon sundowner
  • Maximum 6 guests per vehicle
R1,200per person

Elephant Encounter Walk

2 hours · Sunrise departure

Walk alongside our resident elephant herd under the guidance of our elephant ecologist — a profound experience that will stay with you for a lifetime.

  • Led by elephant behaviour specialist
  • Maximum 4 guests (very intimate)
  • Conservation briefing included
  • Photography guidance on request
R2,500per person

Bush Walk & Tracking

3 hours · Early morning departure

Leave the vehicle behind and experience the bush on foot. Learn to read tracks, identify plants, and understand the ecosystem in extraordinary detail.

  • Armed & qualified field guide
  • Maximum 8 guests
  • Hearty bush breakfast at trail's end
  • Suitable for adults and teens 14+
R980per person

Night Drive & Star Safari

3 hours · After-dinner departure

The African bush transforms after dark. Spot nocturnal predators by spotlight, then stop under a canopy of stars for a guided astronomy session in total darkness.

  • Spotlight & thermal camera included
  • Professional telescope & star charts
  • Hot drinks and bush snacks
  • Available year-round
R1,400per person
